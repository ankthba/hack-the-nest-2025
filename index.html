What is ALS?

Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis is a progressive disease that damages nerve cells in the brain and spinal cord, leading to muscle weakness and loss of motor control. As the nerves die, muscles can no longer function, affecting movement, speaking, swallowing, and breathing. Thereâ€™s no cure, and the cause is often unknown.

Solution

People with ALS often lose their ability to speak and communicate effectively. This can lead to social isolation, frustration, and a decreased quality of life. Many existing communication aids are expensive, complex, or not user-friendly.

We are developing an affordable and user-friendly ALS interpreter that uses Artificial Intelligence to translate sign language into spoken words in real-time. This device aims to enhance communication for individuals with ALS, helping them communicate more easily with just their eyes.
